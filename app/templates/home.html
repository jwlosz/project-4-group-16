Does domestic consumption have any effect on bean export?
Curious to find out if there is any correlation between coffee consumption and coffee bean export, data was condensed and formulated into a scatterplot, returning the following graph.



With a correlation coefficient of 0.47604, it appears there is only a slight positive correlation between the two variables.

Possible theories explaining the correlation include:

  • A country's export type being different than the domestic preference, leading to a larger import of the preferred flavor.
  • An exporting country experiencing a less than ideal crop yield, leading to lower export levels for the season.
  • Market events, such as the coffee crisis of the early 2000s, which affected coffee trade prices internationally.

Ideally, further data would be collected on the topic.
