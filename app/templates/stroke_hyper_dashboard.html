Stroke & Hypertension Dashboard

This dashboard shows the Stroke and Hypertension Datasets broken down by age and gender.



Adjust the above filters to visualize the diabetes dataset.

The above visuals help address questions such as
What are some of the most common risk factors for hypertension/stroke?
Are there specific age groups that see a higher rate of development of a specific condition?
Does gender have an impact on diagnosis rates?.

Some conclusions that we can draw when looking at these visuals are, There didnt seem to be a strong link between age and stroke risk, strokes could occur at any age within the range, with the most occuring from ages 25-80 but this would make sense given the sampling population of this data. BMI seemed to be an affecting factor in younger and older people when considered for stroke risk, as the data tightens up in the larger population age range of 20-80 and spreads outside of these ages. When considering smoking, there does seem to be a higher number of strokes is the subject is a smoker. Lastly, all other factors considered, residance type do not seem to have a strong corralation in diagnosis rates.
