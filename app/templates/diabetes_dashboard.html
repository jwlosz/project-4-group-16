Diabetes Dashboard

This dashboard highlights diabetes data used in this analysis. Data can be filtered by age, gender, fruit and vegtiable consumption, alcohol consumption, and if the subject is a smoker.

Adjust the above filters to visualize the diabetes dataset.

The above visuals help address questions such as
What are some of the most common risk factors for diabetes?
Does fruit/vegetable consumption have an impact on diagnosis rates?
Does gender have an impact on diagnosis rates?.

Some conclusions that we can draw when looking at these visuals are, The is substantally higher rates of diagnosis in agre rantes 55-70. There is a drop off in diabetic rate after 70 but this can also be attributed to people getting older and passing away from diabetes or another issue. When considering gender, there does seem to be a higher number of females with diabetes when they have a higher BMI, which would make sense as an expected result, while men seem to get a diabetes diagnosis at a lower average BMI then women do. Suggesting then men have a lower threshold risk for diabetes then women do. Lastly, all other factors considered, fruit and vegetable consumption do not seem to have a strong corralation in diagnosis rates.
